Background Information About COVID-19 Misinformation

Social Media

Nowadays, fake news is a pressing issue especially in social media platforms. According to a statistics portal that collates information from diverse topics called Statista, about 34% of North Amercans trust the information they find on social media platforms. Unfortunately, social media platforms are the breeding ground for fake news. In fact, about 31% of children and teenagers share fake news online. Indeed, the issue itself have significant impact amongst the community. About a decent portion of Americans believe that fake news lead to a great deal of confusion. Aside from confusion, it causes an unnecessary panic shown through mass toilet paper buying and hoarding of other essential goods at home. In addition, the misinformation in our community leads to a false sense of complacency. For instance, a handful number of people went to the beach at the 4th of July. Hence, fake news is an alarming issue that should be addressed in the community to avoid its dangerous effects.
